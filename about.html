About Me

Some careers follow a straight line. Mine followed a question.

That question — does it matter who sits in the boardroom? — took me from the foothills of Azad Kashmir to the lecture halls of Italy, through the spreadsheets of Pakistani banks, across research institutions in three countries, and into the heart of one of the most urgent debates in modern business: how diversity, governance, and sustainability intersect to shape the organisations that shape our world.

Where It Started

I grew up in Azad Jammu & Kashmir, Pakistan — a place where ambition has to travel far to find its footing. I was the kind of student who took academics seriously, finishing my Bachelor of Business Administration at the University of Azad Jammu & Kashmir with the Gold Medal for the highest CGPA in my entire graduating cohort. Not bad for someone who had no roadmap, only curiosity.

My Master's followed at Riphah International University in Islamabad — on a full Talent Scholarship — where I graduated with Distinction and wrote my first serious research on corporate governance and dividend policy. That thesis planted a seed: governance was not just a technical subject. It was about power, accountability, and who gets to make decisions that affect everyone else.

The Real World, Briefly

Before academia claimed me fully, I stepped into industry. I worked as a Trainee Officer at Askari Bank — a public armed forces-managed institution — designing investment strategies for corporate and individual clients. I then moved to Jubilee Life Insurance, where I managed relationships, hit sales targets, and mentored junior colleagues. I also spent time as a researcher at the Senate Standing Committee of Railways, preparing analysis reports to support parliamentary discussions.

These were not detours. They were lenses. Sitting inside institutions taught me things no textbook could: how organisations actually function, how decisions get made, and how rarely the people at the top reflect the people they serve.

The PhD — and the Question That Would Not Let Go

In February 2019, I moved to Italy and began my PhD at Università degli Studi “G. d’Annunzio” Chieti-Pescara — on a competitive open merit scholarship. My doctoral research examined The Role of Corporate Board Attributes and Corporate Social Responsibility on Firm Decisions. A mouthful, yes, but at its core it asked something simple: does the composition of a board change what a company does?

The answer, as my research showed repeatedly, is yes.

During my PhD, I spent six months as a Visiting Research Scholar at the University of Murcia in Spain, under Professor Antonio Mínguez Vera — one of Europe's leading voices on gender diversity in governance — and an additional period at PMAS Arid Agriculture University in Pakistan. These visits were not just academic formalities; they were the moments my research became genuinely international, tested against different institutional contexts, and challenged by different minds.

I defended in February 2023. Four years of work distilled into one conclusion: diversity in leadership is not a soft value. It is a measurable driver of performance, sustainability, and accountability.

Post-Doctoral Work — From Theory to Evidence

My first post-doctoral position came immediately after my PhD, back at Università d'Annunzio. I dove into one of the most pressing governance topics of the moment: greenwashing. Specifically, I examined how board diversity and ESG reporting practices relate to greenwashing behaviour across listed firms — building and analysing large-scale compliance datasets from scratch.

In an era where “sustainable” has become a marketing word as much as a meaningful commitment, this work mattered.

Bologna — Where It All Came Together

In March 2024, I joined the Department of Management at Alma Mater Studiorum Università di Bologna — one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in the world — as a Research Fellow on a project funded by the EU NextGenerationEU/PNRR programme. The project focused on something I cared about deeply: female entrepreneurship in Italian innovative start-ups.

I built a governance and diversity dataset from the ground up — identifying female board representation, ownership structures, and leadership composition across thousands of Italian start-ups, and examining how these factors shaped firm performance, strategy, and sustainability outcomes. It was forensic, painstaking work, and it produced findings that speak directly to how Italy — and Europe — should think about inclusive innovation.

Alongside my research, I taught. As a Teaching Tutor in Applied Business Strategy at Bologna's Forlì Campus, and in International Management, I brought my research into the classroom — giving students not just frameworks to understand governance and sustainability, but the evidence that makes those frameworks real.

The Research Record

To date, I have published 12 peer-reviewed articles in international journals, including Business Strategy and the Environment, Corporate Social Responsibility and Environmental Management, International Journal of Business Governance and Ethics, Global Business and Economics Review, SAGE Open, and others.

My work spans board diversity and firm performance, ESG disclosure and greenwashing, female entrepreneurship, CEO power and corporate risk, dividend policy, and cryptocurrency herding behaviour. I have been cited internationally, presented at leading conferences including the Academy of Management and the European Academy of Management, and reviewed manuscripts for journals including the Human Resource Management Journal and Corporate Social Responsibility and Environmental Management. Since June 2025, I have served on the Editorial Board of SAGE Open.

I am a funded scholar — my research career has been supported by EU PNRR funding, Italian national research grants, international visiting fellowships, and scholarships from institutions in Italy, Spain, Pakistan, and the United States.

What Drives Me

I study governance because governance determines outcomes — not just financial ones, but human ones. Who sits on a board affects whether a company pollutes or protects, whether it exploits or empowers, whether it serves shareholders alone or society at large.

I focus on diversity because the evidence is unambiguous: diverse leadership makes better decisions. And yet, despite decades of research — including mine — too many boardrooms still look the same.

I work at the intersection of ESG and governance because that intersection is where the next generation of business accountability will be decided. The EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive, the PNRR investment in inclusive innovation, and the growing scrutiny of greenwashing are not trends. They are the new operating environment for every firm that wants to exist in twenty years.

And I believe research has a role to play — not just in academic journals, but in the decisions that companies, policymakers, and investors make every day.

Where I Am Now

I am based in Pescara, Italy, with long-term Italian residency. I am currently working on several projects — examining legal systems and ESG controversies, leadership diversity and green innovation, and the role of AI in individual investment behaviour — while exploring ways to bring my expertise to industry, policy, and practice.

If you are working on questions of ESG, governance, sustainability reporting, or board diversity — in research, consulting, or business — I would love to connect.

“The question was always the same. Only the evidence kept growing.”
