About Me

I am an academic specializing in corporate governance, board diversity, ESG Environmental, Social and Governance disclosure, and the governance of innovation and entrepreneurship. My work examines how leadership composition and diversity, including gender diversity and female participation, shape firm performance, sustainability practices, and entrepreneurial outcomes across established firms and innovative start-ups.

I hold a Ph.D. in Accounting, Management and Business Economics EQF Level 8 from Università degli Studi “G. d’Annunzio” Chieti-Pescara, Italy, and have pursued visiting research periods at the University of Murcia, Spain, and PMAS Arid Agriculture University, Pakistan.

My post-PhD academic career spans two research positions: a Post-Doctoral Research Fellowship at Università degli Studi “G. d’Annunzio” Chieti-Pescara, and a Research Fellowship at the Department of Management, Alma Mater Studiorum Università di Bologna, where I worked on an EU NextGenerationEU/PNRR-funded project examining board composition, governance structures, and the role of female founders and directors in Italian innovative start-ups.

I have also served as a Teaching Tutor in Applied Business Strategy at the University of Bologna and as a Teaching Assistant at Università degli Studi “G. d’Annunzio” Chieti-Pescara.

My Work

Corporate Governance and Board Diversity

My research explores how board structure, leadership composition, female participation, and governance mechanisms influence organizational outcomes, firm performance, and strategic decision-making.

ESG Disclosure and Sustainability

I study ESG disclosure, sustainability practices, and the relationship between governance quality and responsible corporate behavior.

Innovation and Entrepreneurship

My work also examines innovative start-ups, female founders, directors, and the governance structures that support entrepreneurship and sustainable growth.
